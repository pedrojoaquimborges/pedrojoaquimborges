        Take into consideration that the cost of mixing work is somewhat dependent on the musical material, which is why the indicated pricing is an estimation \u2014 in fact, it’s also common for mixing to be priced per hour of work. Generally, mixes tend to cost more when they have acoustically recorded instruments (voice, drums, …) rather than electronic sounds (synths, drum samples, …), a large no. of multitracks or longer runtimes.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        At the moment, I’m running a slightly more affordable pricing on mix\/master\/production services, due to being in the chaotic process of starting to mix and master full-time. On top of that, Covid-19 has taken a heavy toll on many live performers and it feels right to adapt to that.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        If you have questions, refer to the the Q&A links below. If you’d like to chat, email\/message me \u2014 I’d be glad to listen or offer feedback.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
