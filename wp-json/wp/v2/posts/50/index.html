Uma Noite Rom\u00e2ntica com Jo\u00e3o Borsch<\/strong> is a full-length, vivid tour of Jo\u00e3o\u2019s experience as a listener. If its pop vitrine is said to reflect an infatuation with musical icons, the rock-driven inner workings ground the whole record in a search for connection through drama, divergence and impact.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

My own experience with the record is definitely biased, I am happy to find its sound to be, not just impactful and upfront, but respectful of the overwhelming detail in his productions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This record had three clear mixing challenges. First, achieving a striking, impactful aesthetic without making the overwhelming production\/composition detail secondary. Secondly, getting a sound that fits alongside industry-standard pop records while recording remotely, in dozens of different rooms and recording environments. And lastly, getting comfortable going out of the “Mixer chair” every now and then to revisit production details and re-record parts. These hurdles resulted in a thoroughly creative mixing process whose main trait ended up being the ability to embrace a given recording environment’s sonic character and signature \u2013 even if said recording space is actually a group of totally distinct untreatead rooms in different locations.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
